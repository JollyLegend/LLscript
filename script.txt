.SCENE 01 — THE HOUSE GOES QUIET [MOVEMENT]

Location: Exterior — Lakers Lodge, night.

From inside, muffled through brick and glass: a family eating dinner.

Hold long enough to start liking them.

Every light in the house dies at once.

One window comes back on.

TITLE OVER:

LAKERS LODGE

.SCENE 02 — THE LISTING [DIALOGUE]

Location: Inside Leon's Toyota, parked in a lay-by at dusk.

LEON
You made me pull into a lay-by. A mile out. To read it again.

JANE
I'm not reading it in front of the house.

LEON
It's a listing, Jane. It's not going to --

JANE
It'll hear me.

LEON
Right. Can't let the house catch you doing due diligence.

JANE
(reading)
"Overnight occupancy required. Guests must remain for the full night. No exceptions." That's the whole listing. Account's nine days old. No host photo. No reviews. No other properties.

LEON
Rich people are strange about insurance.

JANE
It never asked our names. The confirmation says "the occupants."

LEON
Discreet.

JANE
I asked if anyone else would be in the house.

LEON
And they said something completely reasonable that makes this fine.

JANE
"No one will enter while you are there."

She lowers the phone.

JANE
That is not the same as "you'll be alone."

LEON
You've read it so many times you've turned it into poetry.

JANE
Somebody has to.

She sets the phone in her lap.

JANE
Tell me the real reason we're doing this.

LEON
Five thousand pounds.

JANE
Leon.

LEON
Five thousand is the deposit. First month, last month, agency fee. The flat above the florist. The one with the actual hallway. The one you walked through twice and didn't say a word about.

JANE
It had a box room.

LEON
It had a box room.

JANE
A tiny one.

LEON
For boxes. Or whatever else a future needs storing.

JANE
Don't.

LEON
I'm only saying it had potential.

JANE
I did the maths on my lunch break. Three years of overtime. Or one night in a stranger's house.

LEON
One strange night. Then we take the win instead of interrogating it to death.

Her hand crosses the centre console.

JANE
If we get murdered, I'm haunting you specifically.

LEON
Most romantic thing you've ever said to me.

.SCENE 03 — ARRIVAL [MOVEMENT]

Location: Exterior — End of the drive, Lakers Lodge, night.

Leon and Jane get out of the car and walk towards the house. Leon takes her bag.

Headlights wash over the house. Every window is dark except the sitting-room window.

Then Jane closes the distance between them. Leon takes her bag without discussion.

.SCENE 04 — CROSSING THE THRESHOLD [DIALOGUE]

Location: Interior — Front entryway, continuous.

The front door opens. Leon enters first with the bags.

An ornate wall mirror stands beside the door.

As Leon passes it, the entry light catches the deep burgundy of his jumper.

He does not look into the mirror.

JANE
No signal.

She shows him the phone.

LEON
Rich people pay extra for that. It's called a retreat.

.SCENE 05 — THE COVERED MIRROR [DIALOGUE]

Location: Interior — Hallway and understairs WC, continuous.

A bright tiled corridor. Stairs ahead.

Jane stops at the open downstairs WC.

Above the sink, a mirror-shaped object is covered by fitted DARK FABRIC.

JANE
They covered this one.

LEON
Renovation.

JANE
You don't hem a dust sheet.

.SCENE 06 — THIRTEEN STEPS [DIALOGUE]

Location: Interior — Hallway by the staircase, continuous.

At the staircase, Leon looks up.

LEON
Thirteen.

JANE
What?

LEON
Steps.

JANE
You counted them?

LEON
Everyone counts stairs. They just don't admit it.

JANE
"Guests are asked to remain on the ground floor."

LEON
We're being paid five grand to sleep downstairs. I support the rule.

.SCENE 07 — THE UTILITY DOOR [DIALOGUE]

Location: Interior — Kitchen and dining area, continuous.

Open-plan, warm, clean and unmistakably lived-in.

Jane places her phone FACE-UP on the island. The screen goes black.

At the far side of the kitchen, the UTILITY ROOM door is shut.

LEON
Leave that one.

JANE
Why?

Leon looks at the door as though the answer arrived before he did.

LEON
Probably just the boiler.

JANE
You don't know where the boiler is.

LEON
I have a strong sense of boiler.

JANE
You've never had a strong sense of anything mechanical in your life.

LEON
Cruel. Accurate, but cruel.

As they cross the room, the fixed camera holds the kitchen doorway in frame.

Two columns of faded pencil HEIGHT MARKS. ELIAS and NOAH in a parent's handwriting.

The highest ELIAS mark sits just above Leon's head when he pauses beneath it to look into the sitting room.

JANE
It feels like they just stepped out.

LEON
Maybe they did. Maybe rich people leave their kitchens like hotel rooms.

JANE
There are tea towels on the oven.

LEON
Luxury tea towels.

.SCENE 08 — THE FAMILY PORTRAIT [DIALOGUE]

Location: Interior — Sitting room, continuous.

A warm room.

Opposite it, the FAMILY PORTRAIT.

Four people and a small cream Pomeranian in a garden.

LEON
Imagine being this family. Imagine the dog having a better life than us.

He keeps looking at the portrait.

LEON
That dog has a financial adviser. That dog has opinions about its portfolio. That dog has never once checked its bank balance and felt personally attacked.

JANE
Leon.

LEON
I swear I've seen it before.

JANE
The dog?

LEON
Same judgemental face. Like it's disappointed in me personally.

JANE
You've never had a dog.

LEON
Past life.

A throwaway line. He grins, but the portrait has unsettled him.

At the far end, the gym door stands ajar.

JANE
There are too many mirrors.

LEON
Rich people like checking they're still rich.

JANE
Then why is the little one covered? Still the same.

LEON
What is?

JANE
Your heartbeat. It speeds up when you pretend to be calm.

LEON
I am calm.

JANE
No, you're not.

LEON
You used to do that when you couldn't sleep.

JANE
You knew?

LEON
I liked that you were listening. Felt like I was keeping you warm without doing anything.

Hold on them.

.SCENE 09 — CAMERAS AND CATASTROPHES [DIALOGUE]

Location: Interior — Guest bedroom, night.

A converted downstairs bedroom.

Double bed.

Leon drops the duffel and falls backwards onto the bed, star-shaped.

LEON
I have never been this horizontal.

JANE
It's a bed.

LEON
No. Our bed only does two of the three dimensions.

JANE
That's not how dimensions work.

LEON
It is when you pay rent by the square foot. Come and appreciate capitalism.

The mattress sinks beneath them. Their shoulders touch.

JANE
Five thousand pounds. One night.

LEON
Come here. Worst-case scenario. Go. Full catastrophe. I'll tell you why each one's wrong.

JANE
Hidden cameras. We're a reality show.

LEON
Covered. I swept the room. Smoke detector's clean, no pinholes in the mirror, no lens in the light fitting. I'm an actor, not an exhibitionist -- there's a meaningful distinction and a small overlap.

JANE
You've had two walk-ons and a student film.

LEON
A featured walk-on. I had a line.

JANE
Was it well received?

LEON
Extremely.

JANE
By whom?

LEON
By me. And the director. He said "good," very clearly. Possibly to someone else. But the room heard it. Next.

JANE
Gas leak. We're both slowly losing our minds and this conversation isn't even happening.

LEON
Carbon monoxide detector in the hall. Green light. Also, if I were hallucinating you, I'd have given you fewer opinions. Next.

JANE
It's a cult. We're the harvest.

LEON
Cults recruit, Jane. They don't pay. We are being paid. Categorically not a cult.

JANE
You think tax fraud is more likely than a cult?

LEON
Much larger membership.

JANE
Something in the walls.

Leon opens his mouth with another answer ready.

JANE
Nothing?

LEON
I'll fight it.

JANE
You'll fight something in the walls.

LEON
I'll fight whatever's in the walls. I'll fight three raccoons. I'll fight a raccoon with a tiny knife.

JANE
Why does the raccoon have a --

LEON
Because he saved up. There was a waiting list. Tiny raccoon blacksmith, tiny raccoon fitting, tiny raccoon deposit he nearly lost. He has made a choice to be dangerous, Jane, and I respect it, and I will still fight him.

JANE
What did the fitting involve?

LEON
Very private measurements. Don't mock him. He's worked extremely hard for that knife.

JANE
I'm not mocking him.

LEON
You are. And he's sensitive.

JANE
He's a violent raccoon.

LEON
People contain multitudes.

JANE
(sincerely)
I know the practical things are fine. Something just feels off. Not a gas-leak kind of off. A different kind.

LEON
I felt it too. When we walked in.

JANE
You did?

LEON
For a second. But maybe "off" is just "new." We've lived in a shoebox so long that anywhere we can't hear the neighbours breathing feels like a crime scene.

JANE
It's not the size.

LEON
I know. But it's one night. In the morning we collect five grand, drive home, go back to the flat above the florist and, for once, stop waiting for the other shoe to drop.

JANE
There's always another shoe.

LEON
Yeah?

JANE
That's the thing about shoes. They come in pairs.

LEON
That's my girl. Paranoid and vindictive. I love you.

JANE
I love you too. Idiot.

The mirrors hold them in the same frame.

Neither reflection is ever entirely alone.

.SCENE 10 — THE HONEST TURN [DIALOGUE]

Location: Interior — Guest bedroom, later.

Lamplight.

JANE
-- a very shallow grave behind the petrol station, and you're just whistling along to the radio, contributing nothing -- What?

LEON
Nothing.

JANE
You're doing the face.

LEON
What face?

JANE
The one where you look at me like I accidentally did something amazing.

LEON
You do that quite a lot.

JANE
Name one.

LEON
Right now.

JANE
Talking about your murder?

LEON
The passion. The structure. You've really thought it through.

JANE
I have witnesses.

LEON
You have motive.

JANE
I have an alibi.

LEON
I'm the victim. I can identify you.

JANE
Not from the shallow grave.

Jane reaches up and straightens the collar of his jumper where it has folded beneath itself.

JANE
There.

LEON
Thank you. You're really beautiful.

JANE
You're changing the subject.

LEON
I'm changing the subject.

Holding his hand exactly where it is.

JANE
What if I wanted to keep talking about the petrol station?

LEON
Then I'd listen. For as long as you wanted.

She closes the distance.

The first kiss is gentle.

LEON
You had another point.

JANE
I did.

LEON
Was it good?

JANE
Excellent.

LEON
I could tell.

Leon tries to pull his jumper over his head without completely breaking the kiss.

JANE
Wait. Stop moving.

LEON
(muffled)
This was meant to be attractive.

JANE
It was. Then your head disappeared.

LEON
I was building suspense.

JANE
You nearly dislocated yourself.

LEON
Commitment to the role.

Jane pulls the jumper free.

Leon emerges in the black vest underneath, hair disordered, dignity compromised.

LEON
Say nothing.

JANE
I wasn't going to.

LEON
Your face is saying quite a lot.

Jane smooths the vest back down where it has ridden up.

His heartbeat is already faster.

JANE
Still pretending to be calm?

LEON
Not particularly successfully.

Her hand closes gently around the front of his vest.

The next kiss loses the laughter but keeps its warmth.

Breath.

A smile that neither of them can quite suppress.

The burgundy jumper falls across the back of a chair.

The camera holds its distance.

In the mirrored wardrobes, their bodies overlap. Neither reflection is given a clean outline of its own.

The low structural DRONE begins beneath the room tone.

Jane's eyes flick briefly towards the mirror.

Their reflection is there.

Jane turns away from the glass.

.SCENE 11 — AFTERWARDS [DIALOGUE]

Location: Interior — Guest bedroom, later.

Jane now wears Leon's burgundy jumper, the sleeves covering part of her hands.

Leon lies beside her in the black vest, dark jeans pulled back on with the top button still undone.

LEON
You know what I think about sometimes?

JANE
That raccoon pension scheme?

LEON
How different things could've gone. If that train had been on time.

JANE
It wasn't the train. My shoelace snapped at the top of the stairs. Missed the 8:14.

LEON
And if it hadn't?

JANE
I'd have been a mile away before you looked up.

LEON
Forty pence of cotton holding our entire lives together.

JANE
Somewhere there's a version of me who caught it.

LEON
She's miserable.

JANE
Better commute. Pension. Reads on the train.

LEON
Every morning at 8:14 she feels like she's forgotten something and checks her pockets.

JANE
And you?

LEON
Still on the platform. Waiting for something to happen.

JANE
Forty pence of cotton.

LEON
I'm framing a shoelace.

JANE
You're not framing a shoelace.

She closes her eyes.

The high wide holds after the conversation ends.

JANE
Tell me that was the boiler.

LEON
That was the boiler.

JANE
You don't know where the boiler is.

LEON
I continue to have a strong sense of it. I'll go be brave at a pipe.

JANE
Let's both go.

Already wearing his jumper, Jane pushes the oversized sleeves above her wrists and gets off the bed.

.SCENE 12 — THE FIRST WALK THROUGH [MOVEMENT]

Location: Interior — Hallway into sitting room, continuous.

Leon enters first. Jane is half a step behind, holding tightly to his hand.

They cross and leave frame.

Hold on the empty hallway.

.SCENE 13 — THE HOUSE HAS SHIFTED [MOVEMENT]

Location: Interior — Sitting room, continuous.

The sitting room is unchanged except one dining chair, visible through the open plan, now angles slightly towards the portrait.

The dark gym beyond. The portrait.

.SCENE 14 — PIPES DON'T TAKE TURNS [DIALOGUE]

Location: Interior — Kitchen, continuous.

At night, the window over the sink has become a black mirror.

Leon and Jane enter its reflection before they enter the room itself.

The UTILITY ROOM door.

Then the utility door answers.

JANE
Pipes don't take turns.

LEON
Old houses talk.

JANE
That one answered.

LEON
Then it's rude not to offer tea. We're okay.

JANE
Your heart says otherwise.

LEON
My heart's dramatic.

A few inches open between them.

LEON
Try your phone. Maybe signal came back.

Jane reaches for the phone on the island.

The screen is black.

She sees her own reflection.

The reflection gives her a slow, delighted smile.

Before she can touch the power button, her breath catches.

No air enters.

With the last controlled movement she has, Jane turns the phone over and plants it FACE-DOWN on the counter.

Only then does her hand go to her throat.

.SCENE 15 — THE CARRY [MOVEMENT]

Location: Interior — Hallway, continuous.

Leon carries Jane at speed.

Passes the front door.

Turns directly into the downstairs WC.

He has not looked at the covered mirror yet.

.SCENE 16 — BREATHING FOR HER [MOVEMENT]

Location: Interior — Understairs WC, continuous.

Not a kiss.

The DRONE skips one beat.

Jane drags in a rough breath of her own.

LEON
That's it. Again. You don't have to be okay. Just breathe.

Two people on the floor of the only room with no visible reflection.

Jane looks up at the covered mirror.

JANE
It smiled before I did. How did you know to bring me here?

LEON
I didn't.

JANE
You ran straight here.

LEON
I know.

.SCENE 17 — NOT JUST MIRRORS [DIALOGUE]

Location: Interior — Hallway and sitting room, moments later.

They emerge holding hands.

Leon takes the throw from the back of the sofa and moves towards the large mirror.

The sitting-room mirror.

JANE
Stop. It's not the mirrors.

LEON
What?

JANE
Anything that reflects us. We'd have to cover the house.

LEON
Then we break the one by the door and get out.

JANE
And make a hundred more.

He looks at the glass in the frame.

The hallway light dies.

Every window deepens into a mirror.

LEON
It heard us.

JANE
Or it was waiting for us to understand.

.SCENE 18 — THE FRONT DOOR [MOVEMENT]

Location: Interior — Front entryway, continuous.

Leon reaches the front door and turns the key.

In the black glass, Leon's reflection is already standing with its arm lowered.

JANE
That's what it did to me.

He does not look at the glass again.

LEON
Two-way circuit. Hall light will have another switch upstairs.

JANE
In the dark.

LEON
Together.

.SCENE 19 — FOURTEEN [MOVEMENT]

Location: Interior — Staircase, continuous.

Leon and Jane enter it and disappear from sight.

LEON (O.S.)
One... two... three... Four... five... six...

Hold the empty frame.

LEON (O.S.)
Ten... eleven... twelve... thirteen... Fourteen.

Warm light blooms down the stairwell.

Thirteen visible steps below them.

.SCENE 20 — THE WRONG DOOR [MOVEMENT]

Location: Interior — Upstairs landing, continuous.

A narrow landing.

The landing light switch is beside a BEDROOM DOOR.

His fingers almost close around it.

He catches himself and redirects the same hand to the light switch.

The light comes on.

LEON
Down.

.SCENE 21 — SEPARATED [MOVEMENT]

Location: Interior — Staircase and hallway, continuous.

They descend holding hands.

A FLASH BLACKOUT.

.SCENE 22 — THE MIMIC [MOVEMENT]

Location: Interior — Hallway, continuous.

She draws breath to call his name --

JANE'S VOICE (O.S.)
(from the kitchen)
Leon?

Jane's mouth closes.

Her own voice.

LEON'S VOICE (O.S.)
Jane?

The kitchen voice answers exactly as she would.

JANE'S VOICE (O.S.)
I'm in the kitchen.

LEON'S VOICE (O.S.)
Are you okay?

JANE'S VOICE (O.S.)
I'm okay. Come here.

The distant Leon voice shifts location with the acoustics.

LEON'S VOICE (O.S.)
Stay where you are.

JANE'S VOICE (O.S.)
I am. Just come to me.

The Leon voice answers from elsewhere.

LEON'S VOICE (O.S.)
I'm coming.

Stops herself. Her voice has already been taken.

Jane backs into the weak pool of sitting-room light.

The false voices fall silent.

A human shape stops at the edge of the light.

A heartbeat.

JANE
You're pretending to be calm.

LEON
Trying.

JANE
It's you.

She pulls him into the light.

LEON
Your hands are freezing.

JANE
Always.

LEON
Thank God.

.SCENE 23 — THE DOG REMEMBERS [MOVEMENT]

Location: Interior — Sitting room, minutes later.

Leon and Jane sit close on the sofa.

The portrait faces them.

From the gym: a faint DOG WHIMPER.

A metal LATCH lifts in the dark gym.

The garden doors ease open under the wind.

Cold air enters the house.

LEON
On the floor. Eyes shut.

They press flat to the rug and close their eyes.

Then a soft, wet CLICK. The sound a dog makes at a door it knows.

A tail wagging.

Without speaking, they begin the shared breathing pattern from the WC floor.

A thin, grief-shaped WHINE.

The garden doors move in the wind.

.SCENE 24 — THE PATTERN [DIALOGUE]

Location: Interior — Sitting room and kitchen, continuous.

A ROAR through the walls.

JANE
It keeps splitting us up.

LEON
The phone. The stairs.

JANE
But just now, when we matched—

LEON
It lost us.

JANE
Then we do it again.

Jane looks towards the covered WC mirror, then back to Leon.

JANE
It can copy a face. It can copy a voice. It can move us.

LEON
But it keeps looking for two separate things.

JANE
And when it gets one signal --

LEON
-- it can't find the line.

The utility-room door SLAMS shut offscreen.

The gym doors remain open to the dark garden.

LEON
Front door's dead.

JANE
In here it has walls, glass, stairs, our voices.

LEON
Out there it has the dark.

JANE
Only the dark.

As Leon crosses the kitchen doorway, he stops directly beneath the highest ELIAS height mark.

.SCENE 25 — TUESDAY [DIALOGUE]

Location: Interior — Gym, continuous.

Weights and machines stand in silhouette. The old dog bed.

Cold air enters through the open garden doors.

LEON
Tell me something we're doing next week.

JANE
What?

LEON
Tuesday. Boring thing.

JANE
Big shop. Tuesday's the big shop. You'll forget bin bags.

LEON
I never forget bin bags.

JANE
Eleven consecutive weeks.

LEON
You keep records?

JANE
Of your failures, yes. Thursday your agent calls with nothing and you say, "Good, good, that's exciting," because you're too polite to people who waste your time.

LEON
Accurate.

JANE
Friday we get the cheap pizza and watch something terrible.

LEON
Cheap pizza. Something terrible.

JANE
Saturday we go back to the flat above the florist. You stand in the hallway with your arms out like you're measuring it for a future.

Leon closes his eyes for one second and puts the image somewhere safe.

LEON
What do we put in the box room?

JANE
Ask me Tuesday.

LEON
Bin bags.

JANE
You will forget them.

LEON
I will forget them.

They take one breath together.

.SCENE 26 — THE FINAL ATTACK [MOVEMENT]

Location: Exterior — Back garden, continuous.

A motion floodlight ignites. Hard white light.

The wind. The drone.

His heartbeat is racing.

His breath remains controlled.

JANE
(whisper)
Don't lie to me now.

LEON
I'm terrified.

JANE
Good. Stay with me.

LEON
I'm here.

The drone rises, searching.

He gives her the next breath.

One breath.

An INHUMAN SHRIEK tears through the house, garden and ground at once.

A heavy LOCK CLICKS.

.SCENE 27 — THE TRUST [MOVEMENT]

Location: Interior — Kitchen, dawn.

Grey-gold morning light.

The utility-room door is closed.

On the kitchen counter, Jane's phone remains FACE-DOWN where she forced it during the attack.

Jane's hand enters frame and picks it up.

The screen wakes.

ON SCREEN:

LAKER FAMILY TRUST
RELEASE NOTICE: £2,500,000
CONDITION SATISFIED: VACANT POSSESSION CONFIRMED
NAMED BENEFICIARY: ELIAS LAKER

He has not seen the screen.

Jane turns the phone FACE-DOWN once more.

He takes her hand.
